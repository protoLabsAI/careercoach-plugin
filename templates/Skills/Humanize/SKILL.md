# Humanize

A final editing pass that strips the tells of generic / machine writing and leaves prose that
sounds like a specific, confident person. Apply *after* the content is true and targeted — this
fixes how it reads, not what it claims. It layers on top of the Career Coach's `writing-style.md`
(the anti-slop, anti-fabrication core); read that first for the hard rules.

## The de-slop checklist
1. **Cut throat-clearing.** Delete "In today's fast-paced world", "I am writing to express",
   "As a seasoned professional". Start on the substance.
2. **Kill filler intensifiers.** "very", "really", "highly", "deeply passionate", "extremely".
   The example should carry the weight, not the adverb.
3. **De-cliché.** No "hit the ground running", "wear many hats", "think outside the box",
   "results-driven", "proven track record", "synergies", "leverage", "spearheaded" (unless true).
4. **Vary the rhythm.** LLM prose defaults to same-length sentences and tricolons ("X, Y, and Z")
   everywhere. Break the pattern: mix short and long, drop a one-liner.
5. **Prefer concrete nouns and strong verbs.** "cut onboarding from 3 weeks to 4 days" beats
   "significantly improved onboarding efficiency".
6. **No em-dashes.** Commas, periods, or a restructure (house rule).
7. **One voice.** Read it aloud. If a sentence doesn't sound like something you'd say to a smart
   colleague, rewrite it. Match `Agent/experience-reviewer.md`'s voice rules.
8. **Specificity over adjectives.** Replace "strong communicator" with the thing you did and its
   result. Show, never assert.

## Output
Return the edited text plus a short note on what you changed and why — so the candidate learns
the pattern and the same slop doesn't come back next role.
