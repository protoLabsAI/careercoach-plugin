# Story bank — reusable proof & guardrails

A library of your strongest, pre-vetted proof stories in **STAR** form (Situation, Task,
Action, Result). The coach reaches for these when an evidence map, cover letter, or interview
answer needs a concrete example — so you tell your best stories consistently instead of
improvising them under pressure. Every story must be defensible in an interview.

## How to use it
- Keep 6-10 stories that cover your range (delivery, leadership, conflict, ambiguity, failure).
- Tag each with the competencies it proves, so the coach can match a story to a JD requirement.
- Update the **Result** with real, current numbers. Weak stories are worse than no story.

---

## Story: <short handle>
- **Tags:** e.g. `stakeholder-alignment`, `0→1`, `scaling`, `recovered-a-failure`
- **Situation:** the context, briefly.
- **Task:** what was at stake / your specific responsibility.
- **Action:** what *you* did (first person, specific, no "we" hiding your role).
- **Result:** the outcome with a verifiable number and the baseline it improved on.
- **Proof:** link/reference · **Defensible?** yes / caveats.

_(Duplicate per story.)_

---

## Guardrails (things NOT to claim)
List the lines that would fail the interview-backtrack test for you specifically — domains you
haven't worked in, tools you've only read about, metrics you can't source. The coach treats
these as hard stops when tailoring.

- e.g. "Never imply direct people-management before <year>."
- e.g. "Don't claim production ML ownership — my ML work was prototyping."
